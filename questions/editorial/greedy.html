Greedy

  • A greedy algorithm, as the name suggests, always makes the choice that seems to be the best at that moment. We build up a solution piece by piece, always choosing the next piece that offers the most obvious and immediate benefit.

    This means that it makes a locally-optimal choice in the hope that this choice will lead to a globally-optimal solution. So the problems where choosing locally optimal also leads to global solution are best fit for Greedy.
